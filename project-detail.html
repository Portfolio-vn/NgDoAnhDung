Project Overview

From UAV fundamentals to rescue-oriented MVP.

The project began by studying UAV categories, safety principles, flight terminology and community applications. It then moved into practical design: selecting a multi-rotor platform, wiring sensors and actuators, building a servo release mechanism, and testing a camera link for visual feedback.

01

Mission Need

Assist search and rescue teams in locating people faster and delivering small aid packages in open areas.

02

Core System

F450 airframe, Pixhawk-class flight controller, GPS, camera, edge AI computer, 4G data link and servo drop mechanism.

03

Human-in-the-loop

AI detects a possible person, sends image and metadata, then a rescuer confirms before approach or payload release.

Development Phases

Detailed engineering workflow

The personal project is organized as a case study, showing not only the final drone idea but also the learning and prototyping process behind it.

1

UAV Research & Application Study

Studied UAV types including multi-rotor, fixed wing, helicopter and VTOL, then compared use cases such as rescue, agriculture, construction, mapping and environmental monitoring.

2

Component Architecture

Defined an MVP architecture around an F450 frame, 2212/920KV motors, 30A ESCs, 10×4.5 propellers, 4S LiPo battery, Pixhawk-class flight controller, GPS, camera, edge computer and telemetry/4G link.

3

Mechanical Design & Assembly

Focused on frame rigidity, centralized balance, motor symmetry, propeller direction, ESC layout and vibration control. The real assembly photos document motor mounting and structural integration.

4

Camera Link & Vision Pipeline

Tested camera power and video communication. The intended AI pipeline is camera → YOLO person detection → tracker → compressed image alert with confidence and GPS metadata.

5

Servo Payload Drop Prototype

Built the first actuator concept using Arduino servo control. The final UAV concept uses a 9–12g servo and a lightweight 3D-printed or plastic hook with a dedicated 5V UBEC for reliable release.

6

Flight Test, Safety & KPI Validation

Planned test sequence: bench test, waypoint flight at low altitude, camera/video test, AI alert test, then staged payload drops in an open field with geofence and RTL enabled.

Safety & Testing

Validation plan

  • Run bench tests before attaching propellers.
  • Calibrate gyro, compass, RC channels and failsafe behavior.
  • Enable geofence and RTL before outdoor waypoint tests.
  • Keep a visual observer and avoid flying over people.
  • Test payload release only in a clear open area, starting from 200g then increasing gradually.
Target KPI

Success criteria

  • AI inference target: at least 15 FPS at 640px input resolution.
  • Alert latency target: under 3 seconds for thumbnail image and metadata.
  • Navigation target: target-coordinate CEP within 30m at 60–80m AGL on flat terrain.
  • Mechanical target: repeated successful payload release during field tests.
